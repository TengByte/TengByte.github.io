About

I'm Teng Li, a machine learning engineer at Zoom, based in the Seattle area.

Most of my work lives at the point where machine learning meets real production systems. Right now that means building AI agents that face actual users — designing the tools they call, wiring up connectors and MCP servers, and building the eval harnesses that tell us whether any of it holds up outside a demo. The hard part is rarely the model; it's everything around it — the integrations, the failure modes, and the long tail of "spec-compliant but unusable."

Before the agent work I spent my time on the broader craft of ML engineering: training and serving models, wrangling data pipelines, and the unglamorous infrastructure that quietly decides whether a system is reliable. That background is why I care as much about the plumbing as the models.

What I write here

This site is where I think out loud. Three threads run through it:

  • ML & Engineering — how production ML systems are actually built and kept alive.
  • AI Agents & MCP — field notes on tool design, connectors, and evaluating agents that run against real APIs and real token bills.
  • Career & Industry — the longer view on how an ML engineering career compounds, and what's shifting in a field that reinvents itself every few months.

I write to figure things out, and because the notes I most wanted to read — the ones from people running this stuff in production — were the ones nobody had written down.

Elsewhere
